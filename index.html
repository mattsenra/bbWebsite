Angel Hat

This hat was designed during a very tough time for me and my family, and it was knit with you all in my mind.

During this time, we have had such a huge amount of support and love from all of you. I really wanted to do something to give a little back, and show you all just how much it has meant to us.

I named this the Angel Hat because you are all angels to us!

The hat is knit in an aran weight yarn but would work equally as well in a worsted weight. It is sized to fit most adult heads, with just a little bit of slouch - my favourite ! It has a simple textured pattern that works for both men and women, and it a fun quick knit.

I hope you enjoy it!

Duchess of Devonshire

This pattern came from my love of Chatsworth House in Derbyshire. The shawl represents the Cascade water feature in the gardens. The garter and eyelets are the steps and cascading water, with the eyelets increasing as you move down the cascades. The lace edging depicts the water as it reaches the bottom, with waves and ripples ending its journey.

I chose to knit mine in a beautiful gold, which reminds me of the soft golden sandstone of the house. It works beautifully in two contrasting colours, as well as the solid.

The shawl is worked from the top down, with increases on every row and the edging is knitted on which means you won’t have a long cast off row - always a bonus in my book!

This pattern was a joy from start to finish, and I feel very attached to it because of my inspiration. I really hope you enjoy it as much as I did.

Handbrake

This pattern is now FREE, thank you so much to everyone who purchased it - you raised a total of £1350 for Cancer Reaearch. You are all awesome !!

Last year Dan went through chemotherapy, and during that process he wasn’t able to knit. One evening we sat and talked about his favourite knitted item and discussed the outline of what has become this pattern.

We wanted to create something that could be knit for guys as well as girls, and would be the perfect tv or car knitting project.

We hope that people will knit this for friends going through chemo, or gift it to a knitting friend who has been touched by this terrible illness.

The cowl is knit in the round, using just knit and purl stitches so is perfect for beginners and expert knitters alike.

Within the pattern are links to tutorials, along with a special edition Bakery Bears podcast. This is only accessible through this pattern purchase.

Thank you all so much !
